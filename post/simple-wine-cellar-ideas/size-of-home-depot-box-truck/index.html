Size of Home Depot Box Truck#

Now that you’ve got a project on the horizon, you’ve likely thought about renting a Home Depot box truck. These trucks come in three sizes: small, medium, and large. The size of Home Depot box truck you need will depend on the size of your project and the amount of stuff you need to move.

Small Box Truck:#

The small box truck is the smallest of the three sizes. It has a cargo area of 10 feet long by 8 feet wide by 5 feet high. It can hold up to 2,000 pounds of cargo. This size truck is good for moving small loads of furniture or appliances.

Medium Box Truck:#

The medium box truck is the next size up. It has a cargo area of 14 feet long by 8 feet wide by 5 feet high. It can hold up to 4,000 pounds of cargo. This size truck is good for moving larger loads of furniture or appliances, or for moving boxes and other items.

Large Box Truck:#

The large box truck is the largest of the three sizes. It has a cargo area of 20 feet long by 8 feet wide by 5 feet high. It can hold up to 6,000 pounds of cargo. This size truck is good for moving very large loads of furniture or appliances, or for moving boxes and other items.

Rental Rates:#

The rental rates for Home Depot box trucks vary depending on the size of the truck and the length of time you need it. The small box truck starts at $19.99 per day, the medium box truck starts at $29.99 per day, and the large box truck starts at $39.99 per day.

Tips for Choosing the Right Size Box Truck:#

Picking the right-sized box truck from Home Depot is a key to a successful home renovation or remodeling project. Here are some things to keep in mind:

1. Assess Your Project Requirements:#

Imagine yourself walking through your project, visualizing the materials and supplies needed. Jot down room dimensions, appliance sizes, and furniture pieces. This will give you a rough estimate of the cargo space required.

2. Weight and Cargo Type Matter:#

Heavy items like tiles, flooring materials, or furniture require a truck that can handle the weight. If you’re moving fragile items like artwork or glass, consider a truck with climate control to prevent damage.

3. Plan Your Moving Route:#

Consider the route from Home Depot to your project site. Are there narrow streets or low bridges? Opt for a smaller truck if necessary. Double-check height restrictions if you’re driving under overpasses or into parking garages.

4. Look for Useful Features:#

Home Depot box trucks come with various features that can make your move easier. Loading ramps aid in loading heavy items, GPS navigation helps you avoid getting lost, and climate control keeps your belongings at a comfortable temperature.

5. Set a Budget and Compare Rates:#

Home Depot offers competitive rental rates for their box trucks. Compare rates from different Home Depot locations to find the best deal. Remember to factor in the cost of fuel, insurance, and any additional features you might need.

FAQ#

Q1: What are the different sizes of Home Depot box trucks?#

A1: Home Depot offers a variety of box truck sizes to accommodate various project requirements. The most common sizes include 10-foot, 14-foot, 16-foot, and 20-foot trucks. Each size provides different cargo space and weight capacities to suit specific project needs.

Q2: How much does it cost to rent a Home Depot box truck?#

A2: The rental cost for a Home Depot box truck varies depending on the size of the truck and the rental duration. Typically, the larger the truck and the longer the rental period, the higher the cost. Prices generally start around $19.95 for a 10-foot truck for a few hours and can go up to several hundred dollars for a 20-foot truck for an extended rental period.

Q3: Do I need a special license to drive a Home Depot box truck?#

A3: In most cases, you do not need a special license to drive a Home Depot box truck. As long as you have a valid driver’s license, you can rent and operate a box truck. However, if you plan to rent a truck with a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) exceeding 26,000 pounds, you may need a commercial driver’s license (CDL).

Q4: What documentation do I need to rent a Home Depot box truck?#

A4: To rent a Home Depot box truck, you will need to provide a valid driver’s license, proof of insurance, and a credit or debit card for payment. Additionally, you may need to provide additional documentation, such as a utility bill or rental agreement, as proof of residency.

Q5: Can I rent a Home Depot box truck for a one-way trip?#

A5: Yes, Home Depot allows one-way rentals for its box trucks. However, one-way rentals may incur additional charges or restrictions. It’s best to contact your local Home Depot store or check their website for specific policies and pricing for one-way rentals.
