Small Bathroom Remodel Ideas 2021#

Thinking about remodeling your compact bathroom, but not sure where to start? Here’s a guide brimming with innovative and budget-friendly ideas to transform your tiny bathroom into a functional and stylish oasis.

Design Clever Layouts#

  1. Maximize Space:
  • Opt for space-saving fixtures like corner sinks, compact toilets, and floating vanities to create a more spacious feel.
  • Install sliding doors or accordion doors instead of traditional swinging doors to save space.
  1. Optimize Vertical Space:
  • Utilize wall-mounted shelves, cabinets, and mirrors to store toiletries and essentials, maximizing vertical space.
  • Hang towels on stylish hooks or a ladder to keep them organized and within reach.

Choose Stylish Fixtures and Fittings#

  1. Compact Fixtures:
  • Select a compact toilet with a small footprint to save space and create a more airy feel.
  • Install a pedestal sink or wall-mounted sink to add a modern touch and save floor space.
  1. Stylish Faucets and Showerheads:
  • Choose faucets and showerheads with sleek designs and water-saving features to enhance the bathroom’s aesthetics and functionality.
  • Opt for rainfall showerheads to create a spa-like experience in your small bathroom.

Incorporate Storage Solutions#

  1. Mirrored Cabinets:
  • Install mirrored cabinets above the sink or vanity to create the illusion of more space and provide ample storage for toiletries.
  1. Floating Shelves:
  • Mount floating shelves on empty walls to store towels, baskets, and decorative items, adding both functionality and style.
  1. Niche Shelving:
  • Create built-in niches in the shower or wall to store shampoo, conditioner, and other shower essentials, keeping them organized and easily accessible.

Make It Feel Larger#

  1. Light Colors:
  • Use light colors for the walls, tiles, and fixtures to create a brighter and more spacious feel.
  1. Large Mirror:
  • Install a large mirror on one of the walls to reflect light and make the bathroom appear larger.
  1. Glass Shower Doors:

FAQ#

Q1: How can I make my small bathroom feel more spacious?#

A1: To make your small bathroom feel more spacious, consider using light colors, large mirrors, and transparent shower doors. Opt for wall-mounted fixtures and floating shelves to create the illusion of more space. Additionally, use storage solutions like baskets, bins, and shelves to keep the space organized and clutter-free.

Q2: What are some smart technology options for my small bathroom?#

A2: Smart technology can bring convenience and functionality to your small bathroom. Consider installing smart faucets that allow you to control water temperature and flow with your voice or smartphone. You can also opt for smart mirrors with built-in LED lights, speakers, and weather updates. Additionally, smart shower systems offer customized water settings and temperature control for a personalized bathing experience.

Q3: How can I choose the right fixtures and fittings for my small bathroom?#

A3: When selecting fixtures and fittings for your small bathroom, consider factors like space optimization, functionality, and style. Choose compact and space-saving fixtures, such as wall-mounted toilets and vanities. Opt for fixtures with clean lines and simple designs to create a modern and minimalist look. Additionally, consider the durability and quality of the materials to ensure longevity and minimize maintenance.

Q4: Where can I find inspiration for small bathroom remodels?#

A4: There are numerous sources of inspiration for small bathroom remodels. Online platforms like Pinterest, Houzz, and Instagram offer a vast collection of images and ideas. You can also find inspiration from home renovation shows, magazines, and books dedicated to bathroom design. Additionally, visiting showrooms and consulting with interior designers can provide valuable insights and inspiration for your remodel project.

Q5: What are some clever storage ideas for a small bathroom?#

A5: To maximize storage space in your small bathroom, consider installing shelves above the toilet, under the sink, and inside the shower. Use baskets and bins to organize toiletries, towels, and cleaning supplies. You can also utilize wall-mounted hooks and rails to hang towels, robes, and shower accessories. Additionally, consider using a shower caddy or niche to store shampoo, conditioner, and soap within easy reach.
